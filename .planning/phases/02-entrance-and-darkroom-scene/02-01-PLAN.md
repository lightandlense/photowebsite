**Entrance-specific layer visuals — use CSS gradients and shapes as photo-realistic placeholders:**
Since we don't have actual photo textures yet, construct a convincing scene using CSS:

- `[data-layer="sky"]`: Dark night sky gradient (very dark blue-black to slightly lighter at horizon). `background: linear-gradient(to bottom, #0a0a14, #1a1a2e 60%, #2a1a18 100%)`.
- `[data-layer="building"]`: The brick wall appearance. Use `background: var(--color-bg-surface)` with a subtle repeating brick-like pattern using `background-image` with `repeating-linear-gradient` to suggest brickwork. Include a window element suggestion (slightly lighter rectangle) and a street number. Use `::before` or `::after` pseudo-elements for the window detail.
- `[data-layer="door-frame"]`: The metal door frame area. A darker rectangle positioned center-bottom of the scene to frame the door.
- `[data-layer="door"]` (#door): The industrial metal door. Dark steel color (#2a2a2a) with subtle metallic gradient. Position: centered horizontally, bottom-aligned, roughly 30% of scene width and 60% height. Add a small handle detail with `::after` pseudo-element (small bright circle or rectangle as the door handle). `cursor: pointer`.
- `[data-layer="ground"]`: Sidewalk/ground plane. Dark concrete gradient at the bottom 15-20% of the scene. `background: linear-gradient(to bottom, transparent 80%, #1a1610 85%, #15120c 100%)`.
